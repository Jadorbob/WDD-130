Step 1: The Thumbaround

The thumbaround was the first trick that I had learned. It helped me understand the fundamentals of penspinning at their most basic level. You take the spinning mod, and you spin it. This is the best place to start in my opinion! As soon as you have this step down, feel free to move on to step 2!

The ThumbAround is one of the most iconic and fundamental tricks in the art of pen spinning. Its origins trace back to the early days of pen spinning culture in the late 20th century, when students and enthusiasts in Japan and South Korea began experimenting with new ways to manipulate writing instruments. The trick involves using momentum to rotate a pen 360 degrees around the thumb before catching it, making it a stylish yet accessible move for beginners. Over time, the ThumbAround gained widespread popularity through online communities and videos, becoming a cornerstone of modern pen spinning. It serves as a foundation for more advanced variations like the ThumbAround Reverse and ThumbAround Harmonic, cementing its place as an essential move in the sport.
